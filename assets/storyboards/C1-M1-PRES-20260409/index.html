As the hallway rush gives way to one physician alone with the record, the absence of a structured process becomes the real bottleneck. Modern clinicians are working in systems that move faster and grow more complex than the training frameworks most of us were given. The problem is not a lack of effort or intelligence. It is that clinicians are often asked to improvise around friction without a repeatable way to surface it, test it, and improve it safely. That is why innovation in healthcare is not a side hobby. It is a professional response to patterns of delay, workaround, and preventable strain that keep repeating in plain sight. The work in this module begins by naming that reality clearly and creating enough safety to do something about it.

The line without fear of interpersonal penalty explains why psychological safety is foundational instead of optional.
